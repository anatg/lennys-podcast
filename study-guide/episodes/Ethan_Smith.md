## TL;DR
Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the evolution of SEO for LLMs like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity. Unlike traditional SEO where ranking #1 wins, AEO requires being mentioned across multiple citations, making it accessible to early-stage companies. The conversion rate from LLM traffic is 6X higher than Google Search traffic (per Webflow data), and winning strategies include optimized landing pages, YouTube videos, and authentic Reddit engagement—not AI-generated spam.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **AEO vs SEO Head/Tail Differences**: The "head" (high-volume queries) works differently in AEO—you need multiple citation mentions, not just top ranking. The "tail" is significantly larger in chat (25-word avg vs 6-word in search), creating opportunities for ultra-specific questions never asked before.

- **RAG vs Core Model Optimization**: Focus on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (search + summarization) rather than training data. RAG is controllable and shows immediate results; core model changes take years and are impractical to influence.

- **The 5% Rule**: Only 1 in 20 landing pages drive ~85% of traffic. Instead of creating hundreds of mediocre pages, focus resources on the few that will actually perform.

- **Citation Optimization Framework**: Break citations into categories—your site (landing pages), video (YouTube/Vimeo), UGC (Reddit/Quora), tier-1 affiliates (Dotdash Meredith), tier-2 affiliates, and blogs—with specific strategies for each.

- **Information Gain + Typicality**: High-quality content adds something new (information gain) while avoiding being so typical you're clearly rewriting others' work. This heuristic applies to all content, not just SEO.

- **Topics Over Keywords**: Each page targets hundreds to thousands of questions (a "topic"), not individual keywords. Answer more subtopic questions than competitors to rank better.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **Early-stage companies can win at AEO immediately** (unlike SEO which requires domain authority): A brand-new YC company can get mentioned on Reddit, in a YouTube video, or on a blog and show up in LLM answers tomorrow. Domain authority doesn't gate AEO success.

2. **LLM traffic converts 6X better than Google Search**: Webflow saw dramatic conversion rate differences because users are more primed after conversational interactions and multiple follow-ups, arriving with clearer intent.

3. **The obvious spam strategy doesn't work**: Creating hundreds of fake Reddit accounts or 100% AI-generated content fails because platforms police effectively and algorithms detect patterns. What works: authentic participation, saying who you are and where you work, providing useful answers.

4. **Most SEO/AEO best practices are wrong**: The majority of published information is incorrect because people don't do rigorous analysis. Always run experiments with control groups and reproduce results multiple times before accepting something as truth.

5. **Help centers are now growth channels**: With users asking "Does your product do X?" in chat, help centers become critical AEO assets. Move from subdomain to subdirectory, cross-link aggressively, and fill the long tail of obscure use cases.

6. **Google isn't dying, the pie is growing**: Every few years there's panic about Google's demise (TikTok, Instagram, now AI). Historical pattern shows Google's share remains stable while new surfaces add to total search volume.

7. **Wisdom of the crowd requires diversity**: Feeding AI derivatives back into LLMs creates "model collapse"—shrinking diverse opinions into single viewpoints. If "best ice cream flavor" eventually only returns "vanilla," the system breaks.

8. **More AI content exists than human content**: Analysis of 100,000 Common Crawl URLs shows AI-generated content now exceeds human-created content on the internet, yet only 10-12% appears in search results because it doesn't rank well.

## AI & The Changing PM Role

- **AEO is the second-biggest SEO shift in 18 years**: The first was Google's Panda update preventing spam; AEO represents a comparable transformation but with different mechanics (summarization vs ranking).

- **Question research replaces keyword research**: Without truth sets like Google Ads API provides, teams must mine sales calls, customer support tickets, and Reddit to understand what questions people ask in the longer-tail conversational format.

- **AI-assisted content works, 100% AI-generated doesn't**: Study of thousands of searches showed AI-generated content represents 10-12% of results. The future is human + AI collaboration, not full automation—just as 2007's automated spam eventually failed.

- **January 2025 inflection point**: LLM traffic spiked due to increased adoption and enhanced clickability (maps, shopping carousels, rich modules), creating a steep growth curve after months of stagnation.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Demonstrate experimental rigor**: When discussing growth or optimization work, emphasize control groups, reproducibility, and data-driven validation rather than accepting conventional wisdom.

- **Show understanding of system-level incentives**: Discuss how platform incentives shape behavior (e.g., why Reddit authenticity matters, why AI spam fails) rather than just tactics.

- **Balance aggression with craft**: Ethan's combination of "extreme intensity" and "zen craftsmanship" reflects effective PM traits—drive results hard while maintaining quality and presence.

- **Question the conventional wisdom**: Be prepared to challenge accepted best practices with evidence, showing you think independently and validate assumptions.

- **Focus resources on the vital few**: Apply the 5% rule mentality—identify the small number of initiatives that will drive disproportionate impact rather than spreading effort thin.

- **Bridge technical understanding with business outcomes**: Connect algorithm mechanics (RAG, citations, rankings) directly to conversion rates and revenue, as Ethan does with the 6X metric.
