## TL;DR
Logan Kilpatrick shares how OpenAI maintains rapid execution through hiring for high agency and urgency, while keeping research teams intentionally small. He reveals that prompt engineering is fundamentally about providing context (not tricks), and advises builders to focus on vertical use cases rather than competing with OpenAI's general-purpose products.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Context is All You Need**: The key to effective prompt engineering isn't clever tricks—it's providing sufficient context. Models are like highly intelligent humans with zero background knowledge. Just as you'd give a colleague context about a project, give the AI context about your goals, constraints, and desired outputs. This applies to both direct ChatGPT use and building custom GPTs.

- **High Agency + High Urgency Hiring**: OpenAI's top two hiring characteristics. High agency means people see problems and solve them without needing consensus from 50 people. High urgency means acting with speed. This combination allows small teams to "take on the world" by moving fast without institutional barriers.

- **Measure in Hundreds**: Success framework for persistence. If you've tried something 5 times and failed, you've "tried zero times" when measuring in hundreds. Everything compounds through multiple attempts—this mental model prevents premature quitting.

- **Vertical Over Horizontal for AI Products**: OpenAI focuses on general reasoning, coding, and writing capabilities. The opportunity for startups is building deeply vertical applications (like Harvey for legal) with domain-specific knowledge and custom UIs, rather than competing on general-purpose assistants.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **New interfaces will differentiate AI products**: Chat is valuable but saturated. Products that move beyond chat interfaces—like infinite canvas experiences (TL Draw), consumer hardware (Rabbit R-1), or multimodal interactions—will have competitive advantages. 2024 is "the year of multimodal AI" and new UX paradigms.

2. **Prompt engineering is human communication**: The reason prompt engineering exists is that models give you what you ask for—garbage in, garbage out. The solution isn't tricks but giving context, just like effective human communication. Future models will help by automatically expanding sparse requests into high-fidelity descriptions.

3. **Small research teams at scale**: OpenAI intentionally keeps research teams small even as the company grows to ~1,000 people. In GPU-constrained environments, adding researchers is a net productivity loss unless they significantly uplevel everyone else, since each new person dilutes the compute available for experiments.

4. **Build for GPT-5, not GPT-4's limitations**: Each new model opens new capabilities, but don't expect magic—expect incremental improvement in solving existing problems. GPT-4 was first model where capabilities could be reliably predicted from compute. The pattern: initial "everything changes" excitement, then settling into "this is a really effective tool."

5. **Slack enables rapid coordination**: OpenAI's Slack-heavy culture allows instant real-time communication across remote teams and offices. It's faster to Slack someone than walk to their desk because everyone lives in Slack—even Sam Altman's most-used app. This infrastructure enables the speed OpenAI is known for.

6. **Agent future through GPTs**: Current GPTs are immediate (text in, instant answer). The next evolution is true agents: "Go do this thing and let me know when you're done." This mirrors human delegation—you don't expect immediate answers for meaningful work. GPTs are OpenAI's first step toward this future.

7. **Embeddings unlock grounded AI**: Third-generation embeddings (5x cheaper, better non-English performance) enable question-answering systems grounded in your own documentation. You can embed 62,000 pages for $1. This prevents hallucination by tying model responses to empirical facts from your knowledge base.

## AI & The Changing PM Role

- **Engineering efficiency gains**: Engineers using ChatGPT can ship "way faster"—likely 50%+ improvement on lower-hanging software tasks. GitHub Copilot studies provide analogous data for productivity gains. This doesn't eliminate engineers but dramatically accelerates output.

- **Custom GPTs for workflow automation**: Companies are building internal GPTs for specific functions—writing Facebook/Google ads, delivering experiment results with data scientist-like analysis, OKR planning, VC diligence. These automate specialized workflows without traditional software development.

- **Context is the new coding**: Building effective AI products requires understanding how to provide context rather than traditional software architecture. This shifts PM skills toward prompt design, knowledge structuring, and understanding model capabilities versus traditional feature specification.

- **Humans using AI replace humans not using AI**: The competitive threat isn't AI replacing humans—it's other humans augmented by AI tools. PMs who learn to leverage ChatGPT, embeddings, and GPTs will be more productive and competitive in the job market than those who don't.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Demonstrate high agency in examples**: When discussing past work, emphasize times you identified problems and solved them without waiting for permission or consensus. Show you can "hear something from customers and already be pushing on solutions."

- **Show urgency in execution stories**: Prepare examples of moving fast—shipping quickly, making decisions with incomplete information, or coordinating rapid team responses. OpenAI values speed as a core competency.

- **Understand vertical vs. horizontal positioning**: If interviewing at AI companies, demonstrate you understand when to compete on general capabilities versus when to build domain-specific solutions. Show strategic thinking about where value accrues.

- **Bring reproducible examples**: Logan emphasizes the importance of concrete, reproducible examples when reporting bugs or suggesting improvements. Apply this to interviews—bring specific stories with clear outcomes, not vague generalizations.

- **Ask about belief systems**: Logan's favorite interview question is asking what people strongly believe that others disagree with. This reveals conviction, independent thinking, and self-awareness—qualities worth demonstrating in your own interviews.
