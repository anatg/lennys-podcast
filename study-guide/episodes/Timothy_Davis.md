## TL;DR
Timothy Davis shares tactical advice on performance marketing, from when to start investing (everyone should do paid search), how to run "signs of life" tests on platforms, and what metrics actually matter beyond vanity numbers. He emphasizes hiring data-driven generalists first, building creative flywheels, and focusing on signal versus noise in the overwhelming amount of platform data available.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Signal vs. Noise Framework**: Focus on metrics that directly relate to your campaign goals (conversions for conversion campaigns, reach for awareness) rather than getting distracted by all available data. Most platforms provide too much information—the skill is identifying what matters.

- **Forward Thinking, Backwards Planning**: Start with your end goal (e.g., "be on all platforms" or "reach 1,000 conversions/month"), then work backwards to identify milestones and what you can realistically do now. Example: Start with search (content + keywords only), then add creative for social, then scale to emerging channels.

- **Signs of Life Testing**: Run small budget tests on new platforms to detect if there's potential before building full campaigns. Look for basic engagement signals, not immediate ROI. If you see signs of life, then invest in proper creative, targeting, and campaign structure.

- **Ops Cadence System**: Create a spreadsheet with "rocks" (big categories: finance, performance, keywords, etc.) and "pebbles" (specific tasks: search query reports, negatives, etc.), then assign frequency (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly). Ensures accountability and prevents things from falling through cracks.

- **Hiring Red/Green Calculator**: Track team capacity quarterly by estimating days needed for meetings, optimizations, reporting, PTO, etc. If you're consistently "in the red" (more work than days available) for multiple quarters and can't cut meetings, it's time to hire.

- **True Competition Metric** (via PPC Hero/Jacob Brown): Don't just look at Auction Insights data—calculate "position above rate when we show" + "times they show and we don't" to identify genuine competitive threats versus ego-driven concerns.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **Everyone should do paid search, even pre-scale**: It's user-driven (requires typing relevant keywords), making it less risky than disruptive media. Start here before Meta, YouTube, or other platforms. The exception: if you lack product-market fit or operational readiness (e.g., can't accept local currency), hold off until basics are fixed.

2. **Start with your own customer data for targeting**: Upload existing customer lists to platforms like Meta to build 1% lookalike audiences—these are most correlated to converters. Test 1%, then 2-4%, then 5-7%. The 8%+ rarely works, but test it anyway with small budget.

3. **Creative drives performance more than people realize**: The Dollar Shave Club video is memorable because of emotion/comedy. If you can make users feel something (happiness, surprise, comedy—not just "emotional connection"), they'll remember and take favorable action. Creative is way underestimated.

4. **Each platform requires different creative strategy**: Don't copy-paste what works on Meta to TikTok. User behavior and mindset differ by platform. Test platform-specific approaches. What worked for one company: customer testimonials on Meta succeeded, but needed completely different creative for TikTok.

5. **Quality Score > Bid Strategy for Google Ads**: Instead of obsessing over bids, focus on improving ad strength (excellent vs. poor ratings Google provides), landing page experience, and ad relevance. A single "poor" ad at $7 CPC can drag down your entire account-level quality score—just pause it.

6. **Agencies are good for starting, not scaling**: They use playbooks and lack time to go deep into your specific account. Good for getting started, but plan the transition milestone early (e.g., "when we hit $50K/month spend, we hire in-house"). Have this conversation upfront with consultants/agencies.

7. **Multi-touch attribution with time decay is most realistic**: Users forget where they first found your brand. Give first-touch some credit, but more recent touchpoints drove the conversion. That said, all attribution is biased—run incrementality tests (Geo experiments, Conversion Lift) to understand true impact.

8. **Video creative is performing extremely well right now**: If you have the creative flywheel to produce video consistently, prioritize it on YouTube and other platforms. Start with emotion (comedy, happiness) to create memorable connections. Repurpose video across connected TV and podcasts.

9. **Don't hire until someone "screams uncle"**: Use the capacity calculator to avoid bloat. If the team is red for one quarter due to travel/events, wait. If red for two+ quarters and you've cut unnecessary meetings, then build the business case for a new hire with clear responsibilities.

10. **Train new hires hardcore for 30-45 day impact**: Don't slow-roll onboarding with handbooks and intro weeks. Give them real responsibility immediately (e.g., "Here's last year's campaign performance, here are your goals—go"). Show them your exact workflows by screen-sharing. Make expectations crystal clear. People ramp faster when trusted and given ownership early.

## AI & The Changing PM Role

- **Smart Bidding is already AI**: Google Ads has used AI for years via automated bidding, ad copy recommendations, and dynamic ad builders. The conversation shouldn't be "what are we doing about AI?" but "how are we using the AI already in platforms more effectively?"

- **Creative generation is the frontier**: AI is starting to bridge the gap for scrappy teams without dedicated creatives. Google's dynamic ad builder and Meta's background testing for product images show the direction. Timothy expects more AI tools to automate creative iteration based on performance data.

- **Opportunity for data analysis automation**: Timothy is actively looking for AI tools that can take platform exports and create the visualizations/reports he currently builds manually (quality score breakdowns, click share vs. impression share, etc.). This remains a gap in the market.

- **Potential for malicious automation**: API-connected AI could theoretically automate constant ad copy variations to slip past platform moderation (e.g., trademarked terms), getting "little drips" of impressions that add up. Human execution would be too slow, but AI could make it feasible.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Data storytelling is critical**: When asked about campaign performance, don't just list metrics—explain what signal matters for the goal and what's noise. Show you can filter overwhelming data to actionable insights (like Timothy's quality score visualizations).

- **Demonstrate systematic thinking**: Describe frameworks like ops cadence (regular audits of keywords, negatives, ad copy) or capacity planning (red/green calculator). PMs who bring structure to chaos stand out.

- **Show cross-functional collaboration**: Timothy partners with finance on CAC guardrails, PMMs on messaging, data scientists on incrementality. In interviews, highlight how you'd work with creatives, analysts, and leadership to optimize holistically.

- **Emphasize learning velocity over perfection**: Timothy's "winning or learning" philosophy and testing approach (signs of life, then iterate) mirrors PM experimentation mindset. Frame past "failures" as learnings that informed better decisions.

- **Prove you can coach and scale teams**: Discuss how you'd onboard someone in 30-45 days (clear expectations, hands-on training, early ownership) versus slow-rolling for 90 days. Shows leadership and urgency for impact.

- **Use concrete examples with numbers**: Timothy constantly references specific campaigns, conversion lifts, and metric improvements. In interviews, quantify impact ("increased CTR by X%, improved quality score from 6 to 9, reduced CAC by $Y").

- **Know when NOT to invest**: His advice against paid marketing pre-PMF or with operational gaps (currency issues in Africa example) shows strategic judgment. In interviews, demonstrate you know when to say "not yet" and what must be true first.
