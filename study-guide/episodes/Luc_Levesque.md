# Luc Levesque — Chief Growth Officer at Shopify, Former Facebook Growth Leader

## Guest Profile
- **Name**: Luc Levesque
- **Role/Company**: Chief Growth Officer at Shopify; previously recruited personally by Mark Zuckerberg to lead growth for Facebook Messenger, Instagram, and WhatsApp
- **Background**: VP of Growth and GM at Tripadvisor; founded and sold TravelPod (travel blogging platform) to Tripadvisor; growth advisor to Twitter, Pinterest, Patreon, Thumbtack, and Canva; recognized as one of the earliest SEO innovators in tech, building growth playbooks that became industry standards

## TL;DR
Luc Levesque shares his playbook for growth leadership, emphasizing that impact—not activity—is the ultimate measure of success. He provides tactical advice on hiring growth advisors (structure deals with 3-month cliffs, front-load vesting, prefer equity for alignment), building world-class growth teams (look for "signs of excellence" and past performance), and navigating the massive SEO disruption coming from ChatGPT and AI-powered search results.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Impact Over Activity**: Facebook's laser focus on outcomes rather than effort. Measure performance, strategies, and team contributions solely by their impact on the mission. Ask "What is the impact?" not "How hard did you work?"

- **Signs of Excellence Framework**: When hiring, look for 3+ signals that someone has done exceptional things (work-related or not). Best predictor: a previous boss left their company and came back specifically to poach this person—demonstrates proven performance and willingness to stake reputation on them.

- **Three-Phase Hiring Playbook**: (1) Finding talent: focus on signs of excellence and past performance; (2) Assessing talent: if you don't know growth yourself, recruit existing advisors or growth experts to vet candidates—it's an easy ask that adds massive value; (3) Closing talent: involve the executive team, make it personal by including spouse/family, and be absolutely relentless (Luc's recruitment by Zuck took 7 months).

- **Growth Advisor Selection Model**: Think like an investor. Use a spreadsheet with criteria to evaluate likelihood of company success, exit, and liquidity. Structure deals with equity for alignment, 3-month cliffs to de-risk both sides, and front-loaded vesting since advisors should deliver maximum value quickly and train internal teams.

- **Two Categories of SEO Sites**: (1) Small sites (dozens of pages): require content strategy, editorial work, and SEO optimization of existing pages; (2) Large sites (thousands to millions of pages): user-generated content or marketplaces with viral/SEO loop integration (LinkedIn profile pages as example—viral loop feeds SEO loop).

- **The YOLO Principle**: Not everything needs to be experimented. When you have strong conviction or it's obviously better UX, ship it and measure pre-post or with holdouts. Speed can outweigh the cost of rigorous experimentation—if 40 YOLOs yield 3 wins, you're ahead.

- **Single Keyword Kingdoms**: Entire industries and companies are built on ranking #1 for single keywords. Don't think linearly about growth channels—one strong loop or one dominant keyword position can be company-defining.

- **Morning Routine Bootloader**: Structured 5am routine: cardio/exercise, stretching, meditation, cold plunge, reading, then 1 hour of structured self-reflection using a dashboard tracking personal and professional goals with red/yellow/green status indicators.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **The 10X Growth Advisor Exists**: Just like 10X engineers, the right growth advisor at the right time can say a single sentence that changes a company's trajectory. Luc has seen hundreds to over 1,000% lift from one conversation—something I've experienced several times where there's "a slide in the next board deck trying to explain what happened."

2. **Growth Advisors Should Make Themselves Obsolete**: Structure equity vesting early (not over 4 years) because advisors should deliver maximum value fast, train your team thoroughly, and transfer all knowledge internally. Use 3-month cliffs so both parties can walk away if it's not working—creates perfect incentive alignment.

3. **Wait for Product-Market Fit Before Growth**: Growing a product without PMF does more damage than good—you're exposing users to a bad experience and burning your market. Exception: if you need users to determine if you have PMF, test in "off-the-grid" smaller markets you can contain.

4. **SEO's Biggest Disruption Since Google's Inception Is Here**: ChatGPT and Google's AI-powered search results with answer boxes at the top will fundamentally change SEO, especially for informational keywords. Traffic that used to click through to sites will now get answers directly in search results. Free SEO traffic may shift to paid, or disappear entirely for certain query types.

5. **Hiring Is the Most Important Leadership Skill**: As you scale, your success is entirely determined by the quality of your hires. Build a rigorous playbook with experiments, track mistakes, and constantly refine. Involve your entire executive team in closing candidates, make it personal (involve spouses/families), and be relentless—both Zuck and Toby maintained recruiting conversations with Luc for months to years.

6. **Past Performance Predicts Future Performance**: Stars have repeatedly done amazing things. When interviewing, map someone's entire journey (professional and personal) and look for multiple signs of excellence. Rarely do top performers lack obvious signals of exceptional achievement.

7. **Growth Equals Whatever Moves the Needle**: Don't restrict growth teams to specific channels (SEO, viral loops, ads). Growth includes zero-to-one product building, M&A, partnerships, onboarding—anything that drives the top-level north star. Build wide strategies, test many channels, then lean hard into the 1-3 that work.

8. **Ask Your Kids for Feedback**: Luc asked his sons, "What's one thing I can do more of or less of to be a better dad?" After a month, his son said, "I want to spend more time with you." Now they have structured one-on-one daddy dates every two weeks. Apply structured feedback loops to all life areas, not just work.

9. **Surround Internal Doers with Expert Advisors**: If you can't hire a world-class growth person, hire an amazing doer who doesn't know growth yet, then surround them with great growth advisors so the knowledge stays internal permanently. Better than agencies, which work with multiple companies and lack deep business context.

10. **Communication Brevity Is a Competitive Advantage**: In a remote/Slack/email world, how tightly and crisply you communicate determines whether your ideas get understood and actioned. Read *Smart Brevity* and apply it religiously—people scan, not read.

## AI & The Changing PM Role

**ChatGPT/AI Search Represents SEO's Biggest Shift Ever**: Google's integration of AI answer boxes at the top of search results (shown at Google I/O) will fundamentally disrupt SEO traffic, particularly for informational queries. Users will get answers directly in search results rather than clicking through to publisher sites—similar to how Google's acquisition of ITA disrupted travel sites with direct flight/hotel search. Luc identifies three keyword types: transactional (e-commerce), navigational (getting somewhere), and informational (questions/answers)—the last category is most at risk. Companies depending on informational keyword traffic should prepare for a shift to paid or dramatic traffic drops within 12-24 months. Luc notes that 50% of his searches are now ChatGPT, not Google, forcing Google to react. The new game: optimize for AI, teach AI what you do and why you're best, rather than traditional SEO tactics. This is "the biggest change since the inception of Google."

## Interview Prep Takeaways

**How to apply lessons from this episode to your own PM interviews:**

- **Lead with Impact Stories**: Structure your experience answers around measurable impact, not activities. Replace "I worked on X feature" with "I drove Y% increase in Z metric by implementing X." Quantify outcomes wherever possible—percentages, absolute numbers, user adoption.

- **Demonstrate Signs of Excellence**: Proactively share 2-3 non-obvious achievements (awards, unique accomplishments, times you were recruited back by former managers, entrepreneurial ventures). Show repeated success across different contexts to establish a pattern of excellence.

- **Show Deep "Why" Understanding**: When discussing growth tactics or product decisions, explain not just what you did but why it worked. Interviewers want to know if you're repeating a playbook or truly understand underlying principles and can evolve them.

- **Use Structured Self-Reflection Language**: Demonstrate that you actively iterate on yourself. Share examples of asking for feedback (from users, team, family), tracking your own performance with dashboards or frameworks, and running personal experiments to improve.

- **Ask "Impact-Focused" Questions**: When evaluating a role, ask "How do you measure impact on this team?" or "Can you share an example of someone having exceptional impact in this role and what that looked like?" Shows you think like Facebook/Shopify growth leaders.

- **Prepare Your Own "Teach Me Something" Answer**: Have a growth or product insight ready that's genuinely novel, ideally something you've discovered through your own experimentation. Practice articulating it crisply in 2-3 minutes.
