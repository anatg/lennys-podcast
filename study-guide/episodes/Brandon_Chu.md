## TL;DR
Brandon Chu shares how writing crystallized his PM mental models and accelerated his career by a decade at Shopify, where the company's unique approach—treating everyone as responsible for product thinking, not just PMs—combined with founder-heavy hiring creates a highly technical, ambitious culture. He advocates for PMs to pursue side hustles to understand the full business context and emphasizes that figuring out decision importance matters more than the decisions themselves.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Decision Importance Framework**: Before making any decision, first determine how important it is. Evaluate: Is it reversible? Does it affect many users materially? Spend all your time on the few critical decisions; use gut instinct or delegate everything else to maintain team velocity. Most decisions aren't as important as they seem.

- **Help Teams Ship Right Thing, Right Time, Right Way**: Shopify's PM job description emphasizes servant leadership ("help teams") over dictatorial control. Everyone is responsible for product thinking; PMs are accountable for outcomes but not the sole decision-makers, especially at IC levels. At director+ levels, PMs take more strategic ownership.

- **Platform PM Psychology Shift**: Platform work has 5-10x longer feedback cycles than consumer product work. You're designing a canvas for others to build on, not the end experience. Success requires celebrating intermediate milestones (alpha launches, API releases) and finding validation in developer adoption, not end-user feedback.

- **Platform Principles Stack Rank**: Before designing any platform feature, define and stack-rank your constituent priorities (developers vs. merchants vs. end-users). Example: Shopify prioritizes merchant independence over developer convenience; Amazon prioritizes buyers over sellers. This guides all policy and design trade-offs.

- **Trust Battery in High-Growth Chaos**: At scale (Shopify grew from 500 to 12,000+ people during Brandon's tenure), formal alignment mechanisms matter less than built trust. Writing externally, shipping results, and getting executive endorsement builds "trust battery" that enables autonomous decision-making.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **Writing forces thinking clarity**: Brandon wrote his most influential posts not as an expert sharing retrospective wisdom, but to crystallize mental models he was forming in real-time. The writing process itself creates the clarity, then releases mental load. Even if posts never gain traction, the ROI is huge for refining how you communicate decisions.

2. **Hire founders for product teams**: 30-40% of Shopify's PM org are ex-founders (failed startups or acquisitions). This isn't just about grit and versatility—it creates deep empathy for customers (merchants and developers building businesses) and brings ambition to a Canadian tech culture historically lacking global success stories.

3. **The importance hierarchy beats everything**: Most PMs face hundreds of decisions; being human means you can only prioritize a few. Developing skill at triaging decision importance (reversibility, user impact, strategic alignment) is more valuable than being right about any individual decision. Spend time where it matters.

4. **Platform PM is a distinct discipline**: Platform product management requires different psychology (comfort with long cycles, indirect validation), different stakeholder management (multi-sided marketplaces), and different design thinking (creating possibility spaces vs. specific experiences). It's not better or worse than consumer PM—it's fundamentally different work.

5. **Remote work optimizes for peaks, not averages**: Shopify went "digital by default" and learned that eliminating offices doesn't mean eliminating in-person time—it means optimizing it. Their "burst" system enables teams to gather quarterly for high-velocity creative work in great locations with zero logistical friction, capturing the best of in-person while avoiding daily commute waste.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Demonstrate founder mentality**: Share examples of taking ownership beyond your role, making hard decisions with incomplete data, and persisting through setbacks. Shopify specifically values this—show you understand what it's like to build a full business, not just a feature.

- **Show decision-making maturity**: When discussing past decisions, first explain why that decision mattered (reversibility, user impact, strategic importance). Demonstrate you can triage ruthlessly and focus energy where it counts. Avoid the trap of treating all decisions equally.

- **Prepare cross-functional collaboration stories**: Shopify's culture assumes everyone thinks about product, not just PMs. Show you value input from all functions, build trust across teams, and practice servant leadership rather than top-down control.

- **Be technical enough to contribute**: You don't need to code production systems, but demonstrate you've demystified technology. Share examples of learning technical concepts, working closely with engineers, or even building simple projects to understand implementation constraints.

- **If targeting platform roles, know the nuances**: Prepare examples showing you understand multi-stakeholder complexity, can design for possibility spaces (not just specific UX), and are comfortable with long feedback cycles. Platform PM interviews will probe for these different instincts.
