## TL;DR
Handshake built a decade-old career platform for students, then launched an AI data labeling business in January 2024 that hit $50M ARR in 4 months by leveraging their network of 500K PhDs and 3M master's students. The key insight: as AI models shifted from needing generalist labelers to domain experts, Handshake's trusted network of top academics became extraordinarily valuable for post-training—creating high-quality data to improve frontier models with zero customer acquisition costs and superior retention.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Pre-training vs. Post-training**: Pre-training = absorbing all existing internet knowledge (asymptoting gains). Post-training = creating new expert data to improve specific capabilities (where current gains come from). Labs now focus on post-training across domains like coding, math, physics, law, finance using expert-generated data.

- **The Expert Data Moat**: In human data labeling, "the only moat is access to an audience." Quality requires: (1) Zero/low CAC to experts, (2) High conversion through trust/brand, (3) Superior retention through excellent experience. Competitors spend tens of millions on ads and have 200 recruiters—unsustainable unit economics.

- **Quality, Volume, Speed Triangle**: Model builders care about three things in order: (1) Quality—bad data is worse than no data, (2) Volume—need thousands of examples in advanced domains, (3) Speed—researchers run multiple experiments, need to scale winners fast and kill losers.

- **Separate Everything for Zero-to-One**: When incubating inside existing company: separate team, separate office space, separate metrics/cadence, separate recruiting, separate onboarding, separate all-hands. Different compensation tied to new business. Founder stays hands-on. Take top talent from core business but make expectations explicit (weekends, late nights).

- **Leave Nothing to Chance**: In moments of unlimited demand/unique opportunity, maximize execution. Get on planes for customers, make late-night pushes, check data 6x, ship extra features. "How do you make sure three months from now, six months, you have no regrets?"

- **LTV/CAC in Expert Marketplaces**: LTV = retention × project participation rate. Handshake wins through: zero CAC (built-in audience), high conversion (trust/brand), high retention (treating experts well, quality training). Competitors burn capital on acquisition with poor retention.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **The shift from generalists to experts is the AI data story**: Models got so good that low-cost international labor drawing bounding boxes became obsolete. Now frontier labs need PhDs in physics, chemistry, biology to break models and create step-by-step reasoning data. The market fundamentally transformed from commodity labor to specialized expertise.

2. **Strategic advantages compound unexpectedly**: Handshake spent 10 years building trust with universities and students for career placement. That network became extraordinarily valuable when AI labs needed experts—creating zero-CAC advantage competitors can't replicate. Long-term platform investments create optionality for unexpected pivots.

3. **Post-training is finding where models break**: Expert work isn't just labeling—it's breaking frontier models in advanced domains where average people can't expose failures. PhDs prove where reasoning fails, provide ground truth answers, create step-by-step corrections. They extend human knowledge that never made it to the internet.

4. **Incubating inside requires radical separation**: Don't let existing company bureaucracy slow zero-to-one. Separate teams, office space, compensation, metrics, recruiting, culture. Founder goes 80%+ time on new thing. Take top performers but be explicit: this is chaotic, weekend work expected, different expectations than mature business.

5. **Speed wins in unlimited demand markets**: When there's more demand than supply, execution speed is everything. "Leave nothing to chance" becomes the rallying cry. Ship faster, check quality obsessively, get on planes immediately, work weekends. Structural advantages (audience access, trust) × relentless execution = category winner.

6. **Treating experts like experts is competitive advantage**: Competitors treat PhDs like commodity workers—no training, transactional experience, high dropout. Handshake built cohort-based training, instructional design, community, treating people how universities expect. This drives retention and quality, creating flywheel effect.

7. **The type of data will constantly evolve**: Current focus is academic domains (STEM), but trajectory data, tool use, professional workflows, multimodal (audio/video) are coming. Synthetic data has a role in verifiable domains but humans will be needed for post-training "for the next decade until we reach full ASI." The need won't disappear, just shift.

8. **AI creates compounding advantages for young workers**: Just as "Google search" was once a resume skill, being AI-native is a superpower. Young people can use AI to do jobs that previously required full teams—one person now handles creative, analytics, posting, all media platforms. They're entering with "Iron Man suits" on.

## AI & The Changing PM Role

- **Entry-level work is being compressed but enhanced**: Junior roles won't disappear but will be radically different. One AI-native person can now do what required entire teams (e.g., social media marketer handling creative, analytics, posting solo). PMs must hire for AI-native thinking and tool leverage.

- **Interns can contribute immediately**: Example: intern had first PR merged afternoon of day one. AI removes traditional ramp time barriers. PMs should rethink onboarding for AI-enabled speed.

- **Voice mode is transforming information access**: Garrett uses ChatGPT voice mode extensively (including for newborn care questions at 2am). PMs should design for voice-first interactions as default, not edge case.

- **Job matching will be AI-reinvented**: Manual resume review, manual cover letters both becoming obsolete. AI interviewers collecting skills, work simulation experiences replacing traditional hiring. PMs in hiring/HR tech must rebuild everything from scratch.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Show understanding of AI's impact on your domain**: Demonstrate deep knowledge of how AI is transforming your specific field (like Garrett understood shift from generalist to expert data). Don't speak generically about AI—show domain-specific insight about where models fail and what's needed.

- **Articulate your unfair advantages clearly**: Be ready to explain "why us, why now" with structural advantages (like zero CAC, trusted audience). Strong answers identify moats that can't be easily replicated. Practice the "only moat in X is Y" formulation.

- **Tell stories about speed of execution**: Prepare examples of moving fast in ambiguous situations—getting on planes last minute, working weekends, shipping in days not weeks. PMs at fast-growing companies need bias for action. Use "leave nothing to chance" mindset.

- **Explain marketplace/platform dynamics**: If relevant to role, show understanding of multi-sided platform dynamics (supply, demand, unit economics). Garrett fluently discussed CAC, LTV, retention, conversion—know your metrics cold.

- **Demonstrate founder mode thinking even as employee**: Show willingness to take ownership beyond your role, work in ambiguity, separate from bureaucracy when needed. Garrett's approach of "not trying to be the boss, just trying to get another smart guy in the room" is powerful framing.

- **Connect work to mission/impact**: Garrett consistently tied business success to democratizing opportunity for students. Strong PMs connect metrics to meaning. Prepare your "why does this matter" story.
