## TL;DR
Sales strategies must fundamentally change during economic downturns: 63% of sales reps missed quota in Q3 2022 (up from 46% in Q1), and 76% of companies missed targets. Success now requires conservative quarterly forecasting with milestone-based reforecasting, modernizing comp plans to reward retention over new logos, prioritizing existing customer relationships and warm intros over cold outreach, and innovating business models (like commission-only or fractional sales roles) rather than optimizing failing approaches.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Milestone-Based Reforecasting**: In periods of uncertainty, set conservative annual targets but establish short-term quarterly milestones that unlock budget adjustments. Pre-agree with board/team on what performance triggers acceleration or deceleration to avoid optimism bias. Example: Plan for 10% revenue decline, but if Q1 hits target, unlock increased spending for remainder of year.

- **Sales Comp Plan Modernization (Retention-Aligned)**: Traditional 50-50 base/commission split rewards new business only, ignoring customer quality and retention. Modern approach: Weight compensation toward long-term customer success, renewal rates, and qualified pipeline quality. Reward reps when customers they signed renew or expand, creating alignment between rep incentives, customer success, and business health.

- **The 5:1 Quota Rule**: Traditional SaaS model sets sales rep quota at 5X their OTE (on-target earnings), aiming for 20% cost of sale. Example: $200K OTE = $100K base + $100K commission requires $1M in sales. This model breaks down when focusing on retention matters more than volume.

- **Warm Intro Velocity Over Cold Outreach**: Cold email/call response rates at all-time lows. Instead, leverage existing customers through in-person events (not mixed customer/prospect events), extract 2-3 warm intros per customer, and critically: get text message intros (not email), keeping the introducer on the thread to ensure accountability.

- **Value-First Selling Through Custom Research**: Go beyond "teaching" or "providing value"—create bespoke analysis using your unique cross-customer data perspective. Example: Sahil's Facebook deal—analyzed all Glassdoor reviews/salaries for Facebook vs. competitors, created 9-page custom report, cold-emailed Sheryl Sandberg with insights about Mark Zuckerberg's approval rating vs. other CEOs.

- **Pessimistic Qualification, Optimistic Presentation**: Great salespeople are "extremely pessimistic internally and optimistic externally." Actively look for disqualifying signals to avoid wasting time on deals that won't close, while maintaining positive external energy. Avoid "happy ears"—hearing what you want to hear rather than reality.

- **Business Model Innovation Over Optimization**: When old models fail, don't optimize—change the rules entirely. Example: Bravado launched commission-only/fractional sales roles (Bravado Flex) when full-time recruiting dried up, creating counter-cyclical growth by matching companies needing revenue with reps needing income without employment risk.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **Sales talent should move to customer success in downturns**: Take your best account executives and reassign them to customer success/retention. Replacing churned customers is nearly impossible when budgets are frozen and cold outreach fails. Patching the leaky bucket matters more than filling it when the faucet is off.

2. **Sales compensation plans are "stuck in the stone ages"**: 99% of SaaS companies reward sales reps purely for new ARR, with zero compensation impact if their customers churn next year. Rep A closes 15 deals with 10 churning = hero. Rep B closes 12 deals with 100% retention + expansion = just "good." This misalignment destroys unit economics and customer lifetime value.

3. **Email is where deals die; text is where they get done**: Cold email response rates at all-time lows. Get text message intros from warm connections and keep the introducer on the thread—creates social pressure for prospect to engage and prevents ghosting. Text feels more personal and urgent than email.

4. **Use customers as research assets, not just revenue**: Your unique value as a vendor is cross-customer visibility. Create exclusive benchmarking, sentiment analysis, and strategic insights from your aggregate data to give customers (especially decision-makers) perspectives they can't get elsewhere. Makes you indispensable beyond your core product.

5. **Every CEO is a full-time salesperson**: You sell yourself on starting the company, sell talented people to join for equity/risk, sell investors to fund you, sell customers to buy an incomplete product, sell press to cover you, sell candidates to join. The core function of founding is sales—optimistic externally while maintaining internal skepticism about what's real.

6. **Growth isn't optional even in downturns**: Companies either grow or die—there's no "survive winter and wait." Shrinking kills morale, investor confidence, and momentum. The energy drain from cutting makes eventual recovery nearly impossible. Must find ways to grow (even small ways) to maintain the energy that startups require.

7. **Sales cycles lengthening dramatically**: Average enterprise sales cycle jumped from ~62 days to ~115 days. Deals going to "no decision" (really meaning "not now") at unprecedented rates. Customers dragging feet on any new vendor spend. Plan for 2X+ longer cycles and higher close-loss rates.

8. **Traditional sales hiring models breaking down**: Full-time sales hiring freezes don't mean revenue needs disappear. Companies willing to pay for results but not employment risk. Sales reps can't find full-time jobs but have skills/networks. Creates opportunity for commission-only, fractional, and contract-based sales models previously uncommon in tech.

9. **In-person customer events drive retention and expansion**: All-paid trips for customers only (not mixed with prospects) in strategic timing (e.g., February when budgets tight) can reduce churn through commitment effects and create dozens of warm intro opportunities during social time. Human connection matters more when budgets are scrutinized.

10. **Modern sales requires deep customer psychology understanding**: Sales when done excellently "doesn't feel salesy"—it feels delightful. Best salespeople obsess over understanding buyer decision-making, status quo bias, loss aversion, and commitment patterns. Reading behavioral psychology (like "Stumbling Upon Happiness") creates unfair competitive advantage.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Demonstrate customer obsession through data**: Like Sahil's Facebook story, show you've done deep research on the company/product using publicly available data or their own product. Create custom analysis that reveals insights they don't have. Goes beyond "I used your product"—show you understand their business better than generic candidates.

- **Quantify impact with retention/LTV metrics**: Don't just talk about features shipped or users acquired. Frame accomplishments around customer retention, expansion revenue, reduced churn, improved NPS leading to retention. Shows you think about business outcomes, not just outputs—critical for senior PM roles.

- **Prepare stories showing pessimistic qualification**: Share examples where you killed projects, deprioritized features, or stopped pursuing opportunities after discovering disqualifying information. Shows strategic thinking and resource allocation judgment. Balances narrative of optimistic vision with realistic assessment.

- **Treat the interview as a sales conversation**: You're selling yourself, but great selling means actively disqualifying bad fits. Ask hard questions about company challenges, team dynamics, resource constraints. Shows confidence and helps you assess fit—interviewers respect candidates who are discerning, not desperate.

- **Have a framework for uncertainty**: Prepare to discuss how you'd approach planning/roadmapping with limited visibility. Use milestone-based thinking: "I'd set conservative baseline assumptions, establish clear success metrics at 30/60/90 days, and pre-define what performance triggers acceleration vs. course correction." Shows you can operate in ambiguity.

- **Emphasize cross-functional relationship building**: Sales success comes from deep relationships. PM success similar—describe how you build trust with engineering, sales, support, customers beyond transactional interactions. Give examples of investing in relationships before needing them.

- **Reframe traditional metrics**: If asked about growth metrics, discuss retention alongside acquisition. If asked about feature velocity, discuss customer success outcomes. Shows maturity to understand what actually drives business value, not just vanity metrics.
