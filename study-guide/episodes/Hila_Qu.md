## TL;DR
Hila Qu shares a comprehensive playbook for adding product-led growth to B2B companies, emphasizing that PLG is fundamentally "data-led growth." She outlines common pitfalls (lack of commitment, missing data foundation, no dedicated team), walks through how to audit your funnel to find leverage points, and explains that activation is typically the best starting place for most B2B products. Most companies will eventually need both PLG and sales motions working together.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **PLG is DLG (Data-Led Growth)**: Product-led growth fundamentally requires a strong data foundation. When you give away free product, you get two things in exchange: broader reach and usage behavior data. Without analytics infrastructure to understand which features correlate with conversion and retention, you're giving away product for nothing.

- **PLG vs. SLG Funnels**: Sales-led growth (SLG) relies on marketing qualified leads (MQLs) based on engagement with marketing campaigns (emails opened, white papers read). PLG focuses on product usage as the leading indicator—users sign up for free/trial, usage drives qualification, and conversion happens either through self-serve checkout or sales (product qualified leads/PQLs).

- **Four PLG Funnel Opportunities**: When auditing where to start, focus on these leverage points in order of common priority: (1) **Activation** – getting users to aha moment quickly, (2) **Conversion** – self-serve checkout optimization, (3) **PQL/PQA Motion** – sales conversion of high-usage qualified leads, (4) **Acquisition** – product-led viral/collaboration features. Activation is the most common best starting place for B2B software.

- **Aha Moment Definition Process**: (1) Brainstorm potential high-value actions with your team, (2) Run correlation analysis on each action against 30-day retention and 90-day conversion rates, (3) Identify actions that most lift these metrics compared to average, (4) Validate through experimentation by driving more users to those actions and measuring impact. GitLab's: two users using two features in 14 days.

- **Finding Leverage**: Always seek the area where relatively small investment yields the biggest results. This creates momentum and empowers future experiments. Don't just fix everything—find the bottleneck that unlocks the most value.

- **PLG Org Structure Evolution**: Start with a core growth squad (growth PM, analyst, engineers, designer). Evolve to a full PLG org with counterparts across functions: Head of Growth Product (product org), Head of Growth Marketing (marketing org), Head of Product-Led Sales (sales org). They collaborate on the same funnel with aligned metrics but report to their respective organizations.

- **Three Buckets of Product-Led Expansion**: (1) Upgrade to higher tier, (2) Buy more seats/licenses, (3) Consume more (usage-based add-ons). Trigger expansion conversations at the right moments based on usage data—expansion is closely related to activation and conversion tactics.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **Most B2B companies will eventually need both PLG and sales motions**—PLG provides volume and broad reach with lower barriers to entry, while sales targets big customers with high-value contracts. Starting with PLG from early on is easier than adding it later to a sales-led org.

2. **Commit to 1-2 years of investment or don't start**—Common pitfalls include treating PLG as just "adding a free trial," not dedicating a team, or doing it because it's trendy. Red flags: thinking PLG equals launching free version, assigning just one person without dedicated resources, lack of strategic fit consideration.

3. **Product usage is the new MQL**—In traditional sales funnels, marketing qualified leads are determined by engagement with marketing campaigns. In PLG, product usage becomes the leading success indicator—which features used, how frequently, with how many team members.

4. **Data foundation must come before tooling**—Before selecting product analytics tools, audit your data instrumentation. Identify key actions in your product, check if they're tracked correctly, document gaps, and establish a data dictionary. "Garbage in, garbage out"—bad instrumentation makes any analytics tool useless.

5. **Activation is usually the biggest leverage point for B2B PLG**—Most B2B software wasn't historically designed for quick self-serve value. Focus on getting users to aha moments fast through warm starts, templates, sample data. Example: Miro drops users into templates based on stated use case within five minutes of signup.

6. **Self-serve checkout should be as easy as e-commerce**—Your B2B conversion process should match the simplicity of buying on Amazon or Lululemon. Common conversion friction: confusing pricing, hidden checkout CTAs, non-localized payment options, unnecessary form fields that only apply to some regions.

7. **Hire an analyst before (or with) your first growth PM**—Without insights and data foundation, growth efforts are directionless. The growth PM needs to be highly analytical (stronger in data/experimentation than traditional PMs), focused on conversion rates and funnels rather than features specifically.

8. **Do a full-funnel user audit to find low-hanging fruit**—Walk through the entire journey as an end user (marketing site → signup → first use → aha moment → purchase attempt). You'll often find surprising blockers: confusing checkout forms, no guidance in-product, unclear value props, or inability to even buy self-serve.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **For growth PM roles**: Be ready to discuss experiments with unexpected results and what you learned. This reveals depth of thinking, customer understanding, and how you handle failure. Hila looks for candidates who launch enough experiments to have surprising outcomes.

- **Demonstrate data-driven thinking**: Growth PMs must be stronger in analytics and experimentation than traditional PMs. Show how you've used data to identify aha moments, run correlation analyses, and validate causation through experiments—not just tracked vanity metrics.

- **Understand the full funnel**: Be able to map out acquisition → activation → retention → monetization for products you've worked on. Identify where leverage points were and how you prioritized. Use frameworks like "do is better than show is better than tell" for activation design.

- **Show cross-functional leadership**: Growth work involves product, marketing, sales, and data teams. Demonstrate how you've socialized resources, aligned stakeholders, and driven results when you don't directly control all resources (especially relevant for early PLG efforts or PQL motions).

- **Know your tools and infrastructure**: Be conversant in product analytics tools (Amplitude, Mixpanel, PostHog), experimentation platforms (Optimizely, Eppo), lifecycle marketing tools (distinct from email marketing), data enrichment (Clearbit, ZoomInfo), and onboarding tools (Appcues, Userled). Understand the difference between data hub (Segment) and product analytics.

- **Articulate early wins**: When starting growth initiatives, focus on finding quick wins that build conviction and momentum. Show examples where you identified low-hanging fruit through audits or data analysis and delivered measurable impact in 1-3 months.
