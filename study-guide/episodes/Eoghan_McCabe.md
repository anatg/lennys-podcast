# Eoghan McCabe — CEO & Co-founder, Intercom (AI Transformation)

## Guest Profile
- **Name**: Eoghan McCabe
- **Role/Company**: CEO & Co-founder, Intercom
- **Background**: Irish entrepreneur who co-founded Intercom 14+ years ago. Stepped away as CEO in 2020 due to health issues (mold toxicity, tick bite), returned in late 2022 just before ChatGPT launch. Previously built products including Quitter (sold for $14K). Studied computer science, worked as software designer. Led Intercom's aggressive pivot to AI-first strategy with Fin, their AI agent product now at mid-eight-figure ARR and growing 300%+ annually.

## TL;DR
Eoghan McCabe returned as Intercom CEO when the company was near $0 net new ARR growth, then led an aggressive AI-first transformation within weeks of ChatGPT's launch. Through top-down leadership, radical cultural reset (40% employee turnover), strategic focus, and bold AI investment, Intercom's Fin agent now grows 300%+ annually and positions them to become the fastest-growing public software company by next year.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **First Principles Thinking for Everything**: McCabe describes creating frameworks and whiteboard diagrams for every decision—from event strategy to product roadmaps. Ask: Who is this for? What's the ultimate goal? What's the mechanism? What alternatives exist? How do we define success? This systematic approach became Intercom's cultural DNA and made their strategy "joined up and considered."

- **Wartime Company Mode**: When existential threat appears, shift from democratic/committee-based decisions to unilateral founder-led authority. McCabe said "we need to become a wartime company"—one person makes brave decisions and owns results. Democracy works in peacetime; survival requires speed and conviction.

- **Values as a Sharp Knife**: Redesign company values explicitly to cut out parts of the organization that won't be effective. McCabe rewrote values around resilience, high standards, hard work, and shareholder value optimization—controversial but designed to reshape the org through quarterly performance scoring against both goals and values.

- **Outcome-Based Pricing Alignment**: Charge 99 cents per ticket resolved—100% aligned with customer value. Rather than pricing from costs (which were initially $1.20 per transaction), price from value delivered ($20-30 per ticket in the market). Built confidence that costs would decline while value remained clear.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **You don't have a choice about AI disruption**: Every major technology wave (microprocessors, PCs, internet, mobile) disrupted all categories. AI is bigger than all of them and will disrupt "in the most aggressive violent ways." If you're not in it, you're getting kicked out. Don't try to thread the needle between old and new business.

2. **Late-stage companies must work like startups to compete**: Young AI companies work 12 hours/day, 365 days/year, use AI for everything ("vibe coding"), and move with urgency. Established companies lose because they won't match that intensity. McCabe: "If founders aren't willing to roll up their sleeves and work as hard as the kids, hire a kid."

3. **Complex pricing reflects unfocused strategy**: Intercom's notoriously hated pricing came from trying to do "all things for all people"—customer service, outbound messaging, sales leads—requiring multiple metrics and tiers. Simplification required strategic focus (pick service), bold revenue sacrifice ($50M+ written down), and customer-obsessed values.

4. **Founder betrayal accumulates invisibly**: Over years, founders make small compromises to placate employees, follow advice, or bring investors along. "A little bit of you dies every single time you go and betray yourself." McCabe's return was partly cathartic reclaiming of founder intuition and unilateral decision-making authority.

5. **Great therapy makes better leaders, not softer ones**: Twelve years of weekly therapy didn't remove McCabe's edge—it removed counterproductive edges (insecurity, being triggered, miscommunication). Understanding yourself deeply lets you own strengths, acknowledge weaknesses without fear, and communicate with authentic connection. "Great therapy is a recipe for brilliant leadership."

## AI & The Changing PM Role

- **AI superiority in "personal" service**: McCabe initially resisted bots as impersonal, but realized AI agents provide more personal service than humans—instant availability, consistent expertise, charisma, for every customer 24/7. Better than waiting 2-4 days for canned responses. "AI is actually superior at the things we describe as personal and human."

- **Repetitive work is first to automate**: Any function requiring repetitive operational work (service, invoicing, onboarding) will be automated by agents. "What a horrible use of a human life" to click buttons for macros all day. AI frees humans for creative, meaningful work that uses their "bright spark."

- **Future orgs are human-agent medleys**: Not simply humans on top managing IC agents. Complex mix where humans may be ICs configuring/monitoring agents, agents may assist executives (AI chief of staff), and organizations become smaller, flatter, more efficient. Humans focus on trust, connection, creativity—things AI can't replicate.

- **Young talent advantage in AI**: "AI is kind of a young man's game." Young builders naturally use AI for everything by default. Older companies struggle because most employees don't use AI tools yet. Hiring young AI-native talent or empowering young employees becomes competitive necessity.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Demonstrate first-principles thinking**: When discussing product decisions, break them into components—who is this for, what's the goal, what's the mechanism, what are alternatives, how do we measure success. Show you build frameworks, not just execute.

- **Show bias toward bold action over consensus**: In transformation or crisis scenarios, emphasize conviction and willingness to make hard unilateral calls. Explain how you'd own results, not hide behind committee decisions. Founders and strong leaders respect this.

- **Quantify strategic focus and sacrifice**: Like Intercom cutting projects and writing down $50M+ in ARR for pricing simplicity, show you understand picking one lane means saying no to revenue elsewhere. Strategic focus requires pain tolerance.

- **Use urgency and competition as motivators**: Reference competitive intensity ("kids working 12 hours/day, 365 days") to show you understand what winning requires. Balance with acknowledgment that it's hard—authenticity matters.

- **Connect AI to business model transformation**: Don't just discuss "AI features." Articulate how AI changes unit economics, pricing models, customer expectations, and org structure. Show strategic thinking beyond feature shipping.

- **Embrace personal growth and self-awareness**: When discussing leadership, acknowledge weaknesses openly while owning strengths. Self-awareness signals maturity. Stories of learning through failure resonate more than pure success narratives.
