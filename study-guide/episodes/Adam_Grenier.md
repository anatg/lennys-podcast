## TL;DR
Adam pioneered the "Growth CMO" concept — a marketing leader who runs campaigns with product-development agility, iterating on brand and channels like a PM iterates on product. He has a rigorous three-part framework for evaluating emerging acquisition channels (channel-customer fit, channel DNA trajectory, company risk profile) and is unusually candid about the difference between burnout and depression in high-growth careers.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Emerging Channel Evaluation (3 Ingredients)**:
  1. *Overlap* — does the channel's strength match your customer's needs AND your company's growth goals? (e.g., Spotify + Clubhouse = audio, discovery, community alignment ✓; Spotify + Paparazzi = photo-first, no alignment ✗)
  2. *Channel DNA* — where are they in trajectory? How will they monetize? If their monetization model aligns with yours, you get preferred access. (Hotel Tonight got Facebook mobile ads alpha because they were a perfect non-gaming case study.)
  3. *Your own company DNA* — risk appetite, bandwidth, and whether you have a foundation on core channels before exploring new ones.

- **Growth CMO vs Traditional CMO**: Traditional CMO thinks brand as campaign. Growth CMO treats every brand investment as an iterative product release — learns, ships next version. Product and marketing must be "married at the hip" in software companies. The four P's (product, price, place, promotion) are obsolete — product *is* the company now.

- **T-shaped Career Expansion via Dunning-Kruger**: Deliberately enter the "valley of despair" on new disciplines. Once you know how bad you are at PR (or data science, or product), you'll hire the right specialist and actually listen to them.

- **PMF Reassessment During Market Shifts**: "Start by assuming you no longer have product market fit." When the market changes (economy, platform shifts), your PMF may be gone even if your product didn't change. The customer base changed, not just the next 10%.

- **Yes, And... (from Improv)**: In cross-functional conflicts, accept that both goals can be true simultaneously, then build on them. The opposite — denial — kills the "scene" and destroys relationship capital.

- **Gift of Details**: Specific, vivid details in pitches, briefs, and positioning give others far more to build on than general statements. "We create content to solve people's deep curiosities the way a biography would" opens a design space; "we create educational content" closes it.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **Emerging channels work ~5% of the time.** Most of the real wins come from existing channels that introduce something new (e.g., Facebook mobile install ads in 2012) or channels that finally reach scale (podcasts, OTT). True first-mover bets rarely pay off.

2. **Don't launch an emerging channel test before you have a core channel working.** Emerging channel exploration is for teams with a foundation. If Facebook/Google isn't working yet, TikTok won't save you.

3. **If a channel's monetization model aligns with yours, you get relationship advantages.** Don't just ask "is this channel hot?" — ask "do they need what I am?" If yes, you can get alpha access and partnership treatment.

4. **Traditional CMOs often fail at product companies because of operating cadence, not skill.** They're great at planning 24-month campaigns; they struggle with weekly iteration. The solution isn't to fire them — it's to teach them agile product development cadences.

5. **Burnout signal: declining adaptability.** When a growth person starts optimizing for "the thing we're supposed to do" over exploration, they may be burnt out. People who chose this career want hard problems — when they start avoiding them, it's a warning sign.

6. **Burnout vs. depression distinction:** Burnout = exhausted but still want the non-work things (improv, friends, hobbies). Depression = cancel the improv class too. Losing motivation across ALL life domains, not just work, signals something deeper.

7. **Therapy surfaces root causes that look like symptoms.** Adam went to therapy for "working too hard" and discovered he craved recognition (youngest child dynamic). Understanding the root cause changed how he thought about career — he stopped judging himself for loving work and started structuring work to get his real need met.

## Interview Prep Takeaways
- **Emerging channel questions:** Use the 3-ingredient framework — overlap, channel DNA, company DNA. Shows structured thinking rather than "let's try TikTok because it's hot."
- **Cross-functional conflict stories:** Frame using "Yes, and..." — both goals can be true, the work is finding where they compound. This is how you answer "tell me about a time you influenced without authority."
- **When asked about burnout/career sustainability:** Adam's framework of distinguishing exhaustion from depression, and using therapy to surface root causes, signals maturity.
- **CMO/marketing leadership questions:** The Growth CMO framing — marketing and product married at the hip, brand as iterative product — differentiates a modern PM/growth candidate.
- **PMF questions:** Mention market-shift PMF reassessment — when the market changes, your PMF may have reset even if your product didn't.
