## TL;DR
Adam built the Growth Competency Model (four quadrants: execution, customer knowledge, strategy, communication/influence) as a hiring and evaluation tool for growth teams. His other major conviction: onboarding is the most undervalued growth lever in most products — it's the only 100%-touched touchpoint, and getting it right moves retention curves 10–25 percentage points.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **Growth Competency Model**: Four quadrants for hiring/evaluating growth people:
  1. *Growth Execution* — channel fluency, experimentation, productizing learnings
  2. *Customer Knowledge* — data fluency, user psychology, creative development
  3. *Growth Strategy* — loop modeling, capital allocation, prioritization/roadmapping
  4. *Communication & Influence* — strategic comms, team leadership, stakeholder management
  Goal is a *balanced team*, not a unicorn who scores 10/10 on everything.

- **PMF for Candidates**: A personal framework for choosing where to work — People, Mission, Financials. Every time Adam shortcut one of the three, it ended badly.

- **Painter / Architect / Surgeon**: Three archetypes of growth hires. Surgeons are precision specialists (SEO, paid). Don't make a surgeon your first growth hire — build foundation first.

- **Opinionated Defaults**: Make it hard to do the wrong thing, easy to do the right thing, without eliminating choice. Used at Patreon to guide creator tier structures (3–5 tiers, $3–5 entry price) — learned from human intervention, then productized.

- **Proxy Metrics for Retention**: When you can't wait 90 days to validate onboarding changes, find leading indicators (e.g., at Patreon: velocity to first patron and first $100 processed predicted long-term LTV).

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **Onboarding is the only 100% touchpoint.** It's the first chance to deliver on the brand promise, and the moment users are most motivated. Don't let it atrophy — revisit when you learn something genuinely new about users, not just for redesign's sake.

2. **Better onboarding can lower conversion and raise retention simultaneously.** Weeding out poor-fit users increases retention of those who pass through. You have to measure both sides.

3. **Hire for customer knowledge + execution first in early-stage growth.** Strategy and communication/influence can be developed; raw execution ability is very hard to teach. Internal transfers often beat external hires because they arrive with one competency already nailed.

4. **Back-channel references are your most honest due diligence.** As a candidate, talk to people NOT on the company's official reference list. As a hiring manager, welcome candidates who ask to talk to your former direct reports.

5. **The PMF test for your employer:** People, Mission, Financials. The moment you settle for 2 out of 3, you set yourself up for failure. Watch the inner workings of the C-suite before accepting offers.

6. **Burnout vs. depression have a tell:** When exhausted, you still want to go to improv class — you just might be too tired. When depressed, you cancel the class entirely. Losing adaptability is the burnout signal in growth roles.

7. **User psychology comes before logic.** Most users arrive with an emotional frame. Address the emotional need before presenting the product's rational value. Surveys and interviews seeking "solutions" miss this — seek to understand the emotional state first.

## Interview Prep Takeaways
- **When asked about growth or onboarding:** Use the "100% touchpoint + brand promise delivery" frame. Show you understand that onboarding is a *retention* lever, not just a conversion funnel.
- **When asked how you hire:** Reference the 4-competency model — explain that you look for a balanced team, not a unicorn. Show that you hire for execution + customer knowledge first in early-stage.
- **When asked about company selection or career moves:** The PMF framework (People, Mission, Financials) demonstrates maturity and hard-won self-awareness.
- **When discussing experimentation:** Mention proxy metrics — explain that you find leading indicators rather than waiting 90 days for retention data.
- **For behavioral questions about failures:** Adam's Wyzant story is a model — mismatch of expectations on growth strategy between founder and hire.
