## TL;DR
Cat Wu reveals how Anthropic ships features in days instead of months by removing process barriers, empowering engineers with product taste, and maintaining laser focus on their AGI safety mission. The PM role is evolving rapidly—success now depends on helping teams move fast, building the right amount of features for current model capabilities (not future AGI), and having the taste to decide what's worth building as code becomes cheaper to write.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **AGI-Pilled Product Building**: It's easy to build for super-intelligent future models; the hard skill is building for current capabilities. Focus on eliciting maximum capability from today's models while guiding users to the model's strengths and patching weaknesses. Ask: "What should this product look like one month from now given current model trajectory?"

- **Research Preview Shipping**: Launch almost all features in "research preview" mode with clear branding. This reduces commitment, enables one-week shipping cycles, and sets user expectations that features are experimental and may not be supported forever. Creates a framework for rapid iteration without cross-functional bottlenecks.

- **Building Block Progression**: Start with making individual tasks successful (core building block), then enable multiple simultaneous tasks (multi-Claudeing), then scale to 50-100+ agents running remotely. At each stage, build infrastructure for the next level: remote execution, verification systems, self-improving feedback loops.

- **Mission Over Org**: Put company-level goals ahead of individual product or org goals. Team members should be willing to make decisions that hurt their own KPIs if it serves the broader mission. Example: "If Claude Code failed but Anthropic succeeded, I would be extremely happy."

- **100% Automation Threshold**: Don't stop at 90-95% accuracy for automations—they're not truly useful until they work 100% of the time. Invest the extra effort to teach Claude your preferences and give feedback until reliability is complete. A 95% automation still requires human babysitting.

- **Evergreen Launch Room Process**: Engineers post ready features in a shared channel, then docs lead (Sarah), PMM (Alex), and DevRel jump in to turn around marketing announcements in one day. This tight, repeatable process removes friction for any engineer to ship quickly.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **Product taste is the new premium skill**: As code becomes cheaper to write with AI, deciding *what* to write becomes more valuable. With tens of thousands of feature requests, the scarce skill is knowing which 10 truly matter and how to build them delightfully.

2. **Roles are merging—hire for product taste across functions**: PMs do engineering, engineers do PM work, designers ship code. Anthropic focuses on hiring engineers with great product taste who can go end-to-end from user feedback on Twitter to shipped product in a week with minimal PM involvement.

3. **Timelines collapsed from 6 months to 1 week (sometimes 1 day)**: AI has dramatically accelerated development. The PM's job shifted from coordinating multi-quarter roadmaps with partner teams to removing every barrier to shipping and enabling concept corners where ideas ship by week's end.

4. **Model improvements eat your harness**: Every new model release requires reviewing the entire system prompt and removing prompting interventions the model no longer needs. Features added as model "crutches" (like the to-do list) become unnecessary as models get smarter. Build ahead for capabilities that don't work yet.

5. **Clear goals cut through LLM ambiguity**: Because LLMs are so general, ambiguity explodes around who you're building for and what problems to solve. Great PMs set crystal-clear goals: target user + specific problem + use case. Example: "Professional developers at enterprises need to safely get to zero permission prompts."

6. **Common sense and EQ remain human advantages**: Models lack tacit knowledge about stakeholders, organizational dynamics, communication preferences, and the thousand moving pieces of product launches. The human brain provides crucial common sense that models don't yet have—though this is closing.

7. **Lean into chaos with low ego**: The most valuable people wear many hats, swap them easily, and are low-ego about what work they do to help the team move faster. Success requires facing every P0/P00/P000 with a smile, knowing you won't be perfect but will do your best and sleep well anyway.

8. **Sacrifice product consistency for speed**: Anthropic sometimes ships overlapping features because they want users to choose winners. This creates user confusion about best paths, requiring more education—but it's the cost of launching many features quickly versus planning everything perfectly upfront.

9. **Hire people who've seen ups and downs**: Anthropic targets people with industry experience who understand what gives them energy and how to maintain it over time. This maturity enables the calm, optimistic culture needed to navigate constant change without burning out.

10. **Actions over chat is the paradigm shift**: 2024 products were chat-based; Claude Code generation is action-based. The "aha moment" comes when Claude *does things* on your behalf, not just tells you what to do. This is the eye-opening difference for users who think AI isn't a big deal.

## AI & The Changing PM Role

- **PMs must help teams ship weekly**: The role shifted from 6-month planning cycles to removing barriers so features ship in days. Set clear goals, create repeatable shipping processes (like research preview), and establish cross-functional frameworks so engineers know when to pull in partners.

- **Metrics and principles replace long PRDs**: Weekly metrics readouts with the entire team ensure everyone understands the business deeply. Team principles document key users, goals, and trade-offs so people make decisions independently without blocking on PM. PRDs only for particularly ambiguous or multi-month infrastructure projects.

- **Evals are underappreciated PM work**: Building just 10 great evals helps teams quantify goals, measure progress, and identify gaps. PMs should jump into evals when features need sharper product definition—the output is concrete success criteria and prompts that improve success rates.

- **Introspect with the model to improve harness**: When models behave unexpectedly, ask them to reflect on *why* they made that decision. They'll reveal what confused them in system prompts, what context was missing, or where sub-agents failed. This curiosity reveals how to fix the harness.

- **Find your 5 trusted evaluators**: Not all user feedback is equally qualified. Identify the handful of users who are exceptionally good at articulating what makes a model/harness combination work. Their rapid feedback becomes invaluable signal amidst noise.

- **Engineering backgrounds help "for the next few months"**: Knowing how hard something is to build aids prioritization—you can ship easy wins in an hour vs. debating them. But valued skillsets change every few months as models advance. The real skill is first-principles thinking to identify team gaps and adapt.

- **Build apps you actually use daily**: Don't just make prototype apps for tinkering—build tools you rely on every day. That's where you extract real value and learn most about AI capabilities. Conversely, don't over-customize your setup to the point it distracts from core goals.

- **Pass tedious work to AI, focus on creative work**: Automate repetitive tasks you hate so you can focus on creative parts you love. Use that extra 20% time for pet projects or initiatives the company hasn't had bandwidth for. This is how to thrive, not just survive.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Demonstrate bias toward action**: Share stories where you removed blockers and shipped fast rather than perfecting coordination. Anthropic values "just do things" mentality over rigid role boundaries.

- **Show you understand current vs. future capabilities**: Discuss how you'd build for today's model limitations while staying aware of where capabilities are heading. Avoid being too AGI-pilled or too conservative—finding the right balance is rare.

- **Emphasize product taste with concrete examples**: Use specific instances where you chose *what* to build among many options, explaining your taste/judgment and the impact. This skill is becoming more valuable than technical depth alone.

- **Prepare for low-process, high-autonomy environments**: If interviewing at AI companies, highlight experience making independent decisions without extensive stakeholder alignment. Show you're comfortable with ambiguity and rapid change.

- **Discuss automation and AI leverage**: Come with examples of how you've used AI to eliminate tedious work and create time for higher-value tasks. Show you "get" how to extract real daily value, not just tinker.

- **Ask about mission and focus**: Great question for AI companies: "How does the company's mission influence prioritization decisions?" Shows you understand mission-driven culture matters for velocity.
