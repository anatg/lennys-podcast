## TL;DR
Zevi Arnovitz, a non-technical PM at Meta, demonstrates how product managers with zero coding experience can now build and ship production-quality products using AI tools like Cursor and Claude Code. Through a systematic workflow of exploration, planning, execution, and multi-model code review, he's created a profitable side business and developed a replicable process that any curious PM can adopt to become a builder in the AI era.

## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

- **The AI Workflow Pipeline**: Create Issue → Explore → Plan → Execute → Review → Peer Review → Update Docs. A systematic seven-step process for building features with AI that mirrors working with a human engineering team. Each step has custom `/commands` that prime AI agents for specific tasks.

- **Multi-Model Code Review**: Use different AI models (Claude, GPT Codex, Gemini) to review the same code, then have your primary model defend or fix issues raised by peers. Models have distinct strengths: Claude is the communicative CTO, GPT Codex is the hoodie-wearing bug-solver, Gemini is the artsy UI designer.

- **Exposure Therapy for Code**: Graduate from ChatGPT projects (light mode, conversational) → Bolt/Lovable (guided building) → Cursor (full development environment). Don't jump straight into intimidating tools; build confidence progressively.

- **AI as People Mental Model**: Imagine AI models as distinct human personalities to understand their strengths and weaknesses. This helps you assign tasks appropriately and interpret their outputs.

- **Code is Just Words**: Don't be intimidated by technical files—they're text documents that can be moved between tools and edited by different models. This reframes coding from scary technical work to manipulable content.

- **Continuous Postmortem Practice**: When AI makes mistakes, ask "What in your system prompt or tooling made you make this mistake?" Then update documentation so the error never recurs. This compounds learning over time.

- **The Learning Opportunity Command**: Custom `/learning_opportunity` prompt that tells AI you're a technical PM in the making with mid-level engineering knowledge, using the 80/20 rule to explain complex concepts without overwhelming detail.

## Top Insights (Timeless)

1. **You won't be replaced by AI—you'll be replaced by someone who's better at using AI than you.** The competitive advantage now is in curiosity, learning speed, and AI fluency, not traditional technical skills or years of experience.

2. **Being a 10X learner beats being a 10X PM early in your career.** When Zevi bombed his first product review at Wix, he realized nobody expected him to have all the answers—they expected him to learn fast. He mapped each senior PM's strength (product sense, methodology, systems thinking) and consulted them strategically, making his success feel like their success.

3. **Planning is what separates serious app development from vibe coding.** Tools like Bolt and Lovable are "super eager to write code" but skip critical planning for complex features. Forcing AI to explore deeply, ask clarifying questions, and create detailed plans before coding prevents gnarly bugs.

4. **AI makes it the best time to be a junior, not the worst.** Despite fewer entry-level roles, when else in history could someone fresh out of school build a complete startup alone? Juniors who embrace AI can operate at levels previously requiring teams.

5. **Using AI isn't outsourcing your thinking—it's harnessing everything that gets you to the right solution faster.** The PM's job isn't always having the right answers; it's delivering value to users as quickly as possible. AI is like having a mentor who's always available and never judges.

## AI & The Changing PM Role

**The Collapsing of Titles**: Zevi predicts that "titles are going to collapse and responsibilities are going to collapse" as everyone becomes a builder. PMs, designers, and engineers will all use AI to execute across traditional boundaries.

**Non-Technical PMs Can Now Ship Code**: With proper workflow and `/commands`, PMs with zero technical background can build production features, create PRs, and collaborate with engineers on implementation—not just write specs.

**AI-Native Codebases**: To enable PM-led development at larger companies, engineering teams should make codebases "AI native" with markdown documentation explaining structure and navigation to agents. This setup work pays dividends in speed.

**The CTO in Your Pocket**: Custom prompts can create an AI "CTO" that challenges your thinking, isn't a people-pleaser, and helps you make technical decisions. This democratizes access to senior technical guidance.

**From Consumer to Producer**: AI turns PMs from consumers of engineering resources into producers of working code. The constraint shifts from "what can we build?" to "what should we build?"

**Learning at Unprecedented Speed**: Building side projects with AI gives junior PMs "reps" in strategy, marketing, architecture, and execution that would take years to accumulate in traditional PM roles.

## Interview Prep Takeaways

- **Use AI as your interview coach**: Create a Claude project with custom instructions that it's your coach focused on making you ready, not making you feel good. Feed it best resources (like Ben Erez's framework), then mock interview repeatedly.

- **Record and get AI feedback**: The feedback loop is typically missing in interviews—no one tells you what you did wrong. Record your mocks and have AI analyze where you could improve.

- **Have AI play the candidate**: For questions you don't have time to practice, ask Claude to give you a perfect answer as if it were the candidate. Learn from studying excellent responses.

- **Build a segmentation game**: Zevi created a quiz app in Base44 to practice product sense (segmentation). Build tools that let you drill weak areas during commute time.

- **Prioritize questions strategically**: Use AI (Comet browser) to analyze question banks and identify most frequently asked questions. Focus preparation where it matters most.

- **Graduate from AI to humans**: While AI mocks are valuable for practice and feedback, actual human mocks (cold outreach on LinkedIn) remain essential for competitive interview prep like Meta PM roles.
